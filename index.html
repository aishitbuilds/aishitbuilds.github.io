Curriculum

The 4-Week Advanced RAG Mastery curriculum

Depth over breadth. Systematic over scattered. Deployed over theoretical. Across 9 live sessions we follow one arc: diagnose your current RAG, systematically optimize it, then deploy and monitor it in production.

Week 1 — Diagnose & establish your baseline
Sessions 1–2
Session 1 — The Production RAG Framework: why demo RAG gets stuck at 60–70%, the 5 failure modes (bad chunks, retrieval, re-ranking, queries, generation), and a step-by-step diagnosis framework. You’ll set up an evaluation harness (20–30 test queries), measure your baseline, and identify your #1 failure mode.

Session 2 — Retrieval Optimization Part 1 (Hybrid Search): BM25 from first principles, when keyword beats embeddings, fusion strategies (RRF + weighted fusion), and live implementation of hybrid search on your system with an immediate 10–15% accuracy lift.
Week 2 — Re-ranking & query optimization
Sessions 3–4
Session 3 — Re-ranking Deep Dive: bi-encoder vs cross-encoder from first principles, when re-ranking actually helps, latency/cost/accuracy tradeoffs, and live implementation of cross-encoder and LLM-based re-ranking on top of your hybrid search.

Session 4 — Query Optimization Patterns: HyDE (hypothetical document embeddings), multi-query generation, and query decomposition. You’ll build a decision tree for handling failing queries and systematically rescue your edge cases.
Week 3 — Chunking & multi-hop reasoning
Sessions 5–6
Session 5 — Chunking Strategies from First Principles: the chunking paradox (small vs large chunks), fixed-size vs semantic chunking, sliding windows, domain-specific chunking (code/docs/legal), and metadata + hierarchical retrieval. You’ll experiment with 3 chunking strategies and optimize for your corpus.

Session 6 — Multi-hop & Complex Query Patterns: when single-retrieval fails, iterative retrieval, query decomposition into sub-queries, and a light intro to agentic RAG with simple ReAct-style patterns and guardrails.
Week 4 — Deployment, monitoring & evaluation
Sessions 7–8
Session 7 — AWS Deployment Architecture: Lambda + API Gateway patterns for RAG, vector DB options (Pinecone, pgvector, Weaviate), and a live end-to-end deployment of a RAG API to AWS (including secrets, env vars, and testing).

Session 8 — Monitoring, Debugging & Cost Optimization: logging and metrics, CloudWatch setup, alerting, cost breakdown (embeddings, LLM calls, re-ranking, hosting), caching strategies, and evaluation frameworks (including LLM-as-judge and A/B testing).
Week 5 — Buffer week & student showcases
Session 9
Final presentations of each student’s system (problem, baseline, optimizations, results, and live demo), distilled patterns that worked across domains, advanced topics to explore next (multimodal RAG, deeper agentic patterns), and joining the alumni community with ongoing office hours and support.

The promise for each week

  • Week 1 — Diagnosis & baseline.
    You’ll know exactly why your RAG fails (diagnosis framework + eval harness + clear baseline).
  • Week 2 — Core accuracy lift.
    Your accuracy will improve 15–25% through hybrid search and re-ranking, plus query optimization for edge cases.
  • Week 3 — Edge cases & complex queries.
    You’ll handle complex, multi-hop queries systematically with better chunking, metadata and multi-step retrieval.
  • Week 4 — Deployed & monitored.
    Your system will be deployed, monitored, and costed on AWS — ready for real users.

Final outcome: 9 live sessions over 4 weeks + 1 buffer week, taking your RAG from a 60–70% “demo” to a 90%+ production system with AWS deployment and monitoring.

If you’re comfortable writing code and have built anything with APIs or basic LLM apps before, you’ll be fine. We won’t spend time on absolute basics, but I’ll give you starter templates so you can keep up even if you don’t know every tool yet.
What if I can’t attend some live sessions?
All live sessions are recorded and shared within 24 hours. You can watch them at your own pace and ask questions in the community or during office hours.
Will we cover specific tools (LangChain, LlamaIndex, etc.)?
We’ll use a minimal set of tools and focus on principles that transfer across frameworks. The goal is that you can reason about RAG systems regardless of which library you end up using.
